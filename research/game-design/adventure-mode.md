## 3. Battle mechanics (how a single fight works)

| Mechanic | Detail | Confidence | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Force = team | A **Force** is an ordered team of Gotcha Borgs. Each Force can hold **up to 30 borgs**; you can store up to **20 Forces** at once. | Confirmed | Search summary (Wikipedia-derived) |
| GF-energy budget | Every borg has a **GF-energy cost** (our roster's `energy`; stronger = pricier). A Force's total is the sum of its borgs' costs. In **Story Mode the energy budget grows as you progress**; in multiplayer the cap is **set by players beforehand**. | Confirmed | Wikipedia; Fandom; StrategyWiki |
| One borg at a time | You **directly control one borg at a time** from your Force. | Confirmed | Wikipedia; StrategyWiki |
| Spawn order / switching | Borgs enter **in the order placed during Force creation**. When the active borg is defeated, **the next one automatically arrives** and continues. *(So "switching" = your current borg dying, not a free swap — confirm whether manual self-destruct/swap exists; Unknown.)* | Confirmed (auto-next) / Unknown (manual swap) | Wikipedia; StrategyWiki |
| Win / lose condition | The **GF-energy meter** (yours green top-left, enemy red) shows the summed cost of each side's remaining borgs and **depletes as borgs die**. **First side reduced to 0 loses** — i.e. you win by defeating the enemy's entire Force, you lose when your whole Force is gone. | Confirmed | Search summary (StrategyWiki/Wikipedia) |
| Many-vs-many feel | Although you pilot one, the arena can have many enemy borgs queued; large missions throw waves. Effectively **wave attrition** between two Forces. | Likely | Fury_Hikari mission data |
| Lock-on combat | **Constant/auto lock-on** to the targeted enemy. | Confirmed | Fandom; StrategyWiki |
| Attacks | Each borg has roughly **five attack options**: a context-sensitive **close-range/melee** plus **~3 ranged ("shot")** attacks, and a **special** (the "X special"). Stats in our roster: `shot`, `attack`(melee), `defense`, `speed`. | Confirmed | Fandom |
| Movement | Free 3D arena movement with **dash**, **jump / air-jump (levels)** and **boost jump / flight** (per-borg, see roster `jump` field; Wing/Valkyrie/Dragon borgs fly). | Confirmed | Roster + Fandom/guide |
| Time limit | **No hard time limit found** for standard battles. One Tuff Stuff duel notes that **stalling makes the rival (Sho) leave**. Treat "timer" as **Unknown / mostly none**. | Likely (none) | Fury_Hikari |
| Tribes & effects | 200+ borgs across **tribes** (Ninja, Gun, Knight, Girl, Machine, Dragon, Bug, Tank, Fortress, Death, etc.); some have utility effects (size change, transform opponent, barriers, healing nurses). | Confirmed | Fandom; roster |

**Battle loop (recreation-ready summary):** two Forces enter an arena → each side's GF-energy meter = sum of its borgs' costs → each player pilots one borg with lock-on, melee + ranged + special, moving/flying freely → when your borg dies the next in your ordered Force spawns → a side loses when its meter hits 0 (all borgs defeated).

## 5. Co-op / multiplayer notes (for our 1–4 player online build)

| Item | Finding | Confidence | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Original multiplayer | Local, **cooperative and versus** modes supported. | Confirmed | Wikipedia; Fandom |
| Player count (original) | Sources confirm **2-player versus** explicitly; broader counts "not detailed." Original is effectively a **2-player local** experience (GameCube 4 controller ports exist but co-op campaign was built around 2P). | Confirmed (2P) / Unknown (3–4P) | Fandom; search summary |
| Multiplayer balance | In multiplayer the **GF-energy cap is agreed by players beforehand**, so both Forces share a budget ceiling — clean knob for our online lobby settings. | Confirmed | Wikipedia; Fandom |

| Healer/support synergy | Nurse/Witch borgs heal allies — explicitly more useful in multiplayer/VS. Good for co-op role design. | Likely | Fandom |

**Recreation guidance:** the campaign's "Cooperate with X" missions already assume a 2-side, team-vs-Death-Force structure. For 1–4 player online co-op, treat each mission as **one shared enemy Force (the listed Death Force waves)** vs **1–4 player Forces**, scale the enemy GF-energy budget by player count, and keep the "defeat the entire enemy Force / lose when all your Forces are gone" win/lose rule.

## 6. Biggest documented gap

**Exact in-game stage *numbering/branch order* and precise enemy *counts* are not authoritatively published.** The only end-to-end mission list is a single fan FAQ (Fury_Hikari), whose mission numbering, branch placement (Tuff Stuff, 28b/28c, conditional recruits), and per-enemy counts are not corroborated by a second full source. The 5-chapter spine and Major Missions are solid; the fine-grained ordering and counts should be **verified against a playthrough / the JP per-location stage pages** (links in sources) before being treated as canon.

Secondary gaps (Unknown): whether the player can **manually switch/sacrifice** the active borg mid-battle, exact **respawn timing**, and any **per-stage time limits**.
